Decision Making and Social Behavior Informed Consent Form

Title of research study: Human Judgment and Decision Making

 

IRB Protocol Number: 18-0511

 

Investigator: Eric Pedersen

 

Purpose of the Study

 

We invite you to take part in a research study because you are 18 years of age or older and are part of either the SONA General Psychology subject pool or the SONA Paid subject pool at the University of Colorado Boulder.

 

The purpose of the study is to understand how individual differences in certain psychological traits affects human decision making and performance across a number of tasks.

 

We do not know of any benefits this research will provide for anyone who participates, other than the knowledge that you are contributing to important scientific research. This research will help researchers better understand human decision making.

 

We expect that you will be in this research study for less than 60 minutes.

 

We expect about 3000 people will be in this research study.

 

Explanation of Procedures

 

This study has two main components. The first component entails a series of judgment and decision-making tasks. This will include individual tasks in which you will be presented with puzzles and games to solve on your own, as well as joint tasks, in which you will participate alongside a partner. In some of these tasks, there will be the potential for you to earn a bonus cash payment for yourself or for others. A bonus cash payment is not XXX

 

The second component entails a set of survey questionnaires. These questionnaires are designed to measure individual and demographic differences between people, and will allow us to see how these relate to performance in the judgment and decision-making tasks.

 

All of the data collected in this study will come from these measures.

 

This research will take place in Muenzinger Hall, Department of Psychology and Neuroscience at the University of Colorado Boulder. This research will be conducted between September 1, 2018, and May 30, 2020. Your participation will last 60 minutes or less.

 

Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal

 

Whether or not you take part in this research is your choice. You can leave the research at any time and it will not be held against you. If you decide to withdraw for the study, any data collected during your session will be erased and will not be used for analyses.

 

If you are a CU Boulder student or employee, taking part in this research is not part of your class work or duties. You can refuse to enroll, or withdraw after enrolling at any time, with no effect on your class standing, grades, or job at CU Boulder. You will not be offered or receive any special consideration if you take part in this research.

 

If you are a CU student enrolled in General Psychology, instead of being in this research study, your options may include writing a short paper instead (consult your General Psychology syllabus for details).

Potential Benefits

 

We cannot promise any benefits to you or others from your taking part in this research. However, possible benefits include bonus payment as a result of your participation on some of the study’s tasks. The bonus payment you might receive will depend on your choices during some of the study’s procedures. These choices might also result in bonus payments for other participants in this study. Importantly, these bonus payments are NOT compensation for participating in the study. As described above, all participants will receive course credit as compensation for participation in this study. Thus, participants who receive bonus payments based on the results of certain study tasks will receive such payment in addition to the course credit.
